UV Rays

What are UV Rays?

Ultraviolet (UV) radiation is a form of non-ionizing radiation that is emitted by the sun and artificial sources, such as tanning beds.


Where is UV radiation used?

They can be observed in the sun, tanning bulbs, fluorescent bulbs, UVB lights, UV invisible ink pens, and Black Holes.


How do you view UV rays?

GALEX is a satellite that can observe UV rays in space. This satellite is not currently active and was shut down in early 2013. Its goal was to observe the history of star formation in our Universe in ultraviolet wavelengths, and it observed over a half-billion galaxies going back to when our Universe was just about 3 billion years old.
